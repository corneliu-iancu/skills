# The 7 lenses — detailed guide

Each lens reframes your question through a different angle. Use one or combine several for comprehensive exploration.

---

## Lens 1: Challenge — What's wrong with my reasoning?

**Core idea:** Most people want validation. Flip that — ask to actively attack your logic.

**Reformulated question:**
- "What are the holes in this approach?"
- "Where would this plan break?"
- "What false assumption am I making?"
- "What evidence would disprove my thinking?"

**When to use:**
- Before implementing a big decision
- When you're too confident
- During planning (catch flaws early)
- Before a pitch or proposal

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "I'm going to focus on paid acquisition for 2026."
- **Challenge lens:** "What's wrong with a pure paid acquisition strategy?"

Might reveal: saturation in your segment, CAC trends, retention issues hiding behind acquisition numbers.

**Prompt to AI:**
```
I'm planning [X]. Play devil's advocate. What are the 3 most likely ways this fails?
Don't validate — challenge. What am I wrong about?
```

---

## Lens 2: Unconstrained — What if I had infinite resources?

**Core idea:** Constraints bias us. What would you do if money, time, or headcount weren't limiting?

**Reformulated question:**
- "What's the perfect solution without constraints?"
- "If budget were unlimited, what would I build?"
- "What would I do if I had 10x the time?"
- "What's the most ambitious version of this?"

**When to use:**
- Strategic planning (vision-setting)
- When you feel stuck in "good enough"
- Discovering what really matters to you
- Reframing impossible problems

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "How do I optimize our API response time?"
- **Unconstrained lens:** "If I could rebuild our entire architecture, what would it look like?"

Might reveal: you'd move to a different tech stack, redesign the data model, distribute globally.

**Prompt to AI:**
```
Forget constraints. If I had unlimited budget/time/headcount, what would the ideal
version of [X] look like? Dream big first, then we'll work backward to reality.
```

---

## Lens 3: Meta — What didn't I ask?

**Core idea:** The questions you don't ask are often more important than the ones you do.

**Reformulated question:**
- "What should I have asked to get a better answer?"
- "What gaps are in my framing?"
- "What edge cases am I ignoring?"
- "What would a wise person ask me?"

**When to use:**
- Completeness checks
- Before finalizing a plan
- When something feels off but you can't name it
- Exploring scope and boundaries

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "Should I hire a senior engineer or two junior engineers?"
- **Meta lens:** "What am I not asking about team dynamics, mentorship, or long-term growth?"

Might reveal: you haven't asked about knowledge transfer, which matters more than raw headcount.

**Prompt to AI:**
```
I asked [X]. Now, what questions should I have asked but didn't?
What am I missing? What would a smart person ask me next?
```

---

## Lens 4: Uncertainty — Where am I wrong?

**Core idea:** Admit uncertainty explicitly. It's more useful than false confidence.

**Reformulated question:**
- "Where am I probably wrong about this?"
- "What's the biggest risk in my thinking?"
- "What would shift my conviction?"
- "What's my confidence level, really?"

**When to use:**
- Risk assessment
- Decision-making under uncertainty
- When you're too confident
- Building resilience into plans

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "Our product roadmap is set for next quarter."
- **Uncertainty lens:** "What am I most uncertain about? What would invalidate this roadmap?"

Might reveal: market shifts, user behavior changes, competitive moves you haven't accounted for.

**Prompt to AI:**
```
Knowing everything you know now, where am I probably wrong about [X]?
What would shake my confidence? Where should I build contingencies?
```

---

## Lens 5: Inverse — What's the opposite?

**Core idea:** Flipping the problem reveals hidden assumptions and root causes.

**Reformulated question:**
- "How would I achieve the opposite?"
- "What if I actively tried to fail?"
- "What would maximize churn/cost/failure?"
- "What's the inverse of my goal?"

**When to use:**
- Finding root causes
- Discovering hidden assumptions
- Creative problem-solving
- Stress-testing ideas

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "How do I build better retention?"
- **Inverse lens:** "How would I maximize churn? What would drive users away?"

The inverse answers often reveal your retention strategy's weaknesses faster than direct questions.

**Prompt to AI:**
```
Instead of [goal], how would you achieve the opposite?
What would maximize [negative outcome]? This often reveals what I'm missing.
```

---

## Lens 6: Reframe — How would a [domain expert] see this?

**Core idea:** Cross-domain thinking unlocks novel approaches.

**Reformulated question:**
- "How would a behavioral psychologist see this?"
- "What would a biologist notice?"
- "How would a designer approach this problem?"
- "What would a systems thinker ask?"

**When to use:**
- Brainstorming (creative breakthroughs)
- Stuck problems
- Learning from adjacent fields
- Finding novel solutions

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "How do I reduce support ticket volume?"
- **Reframe lens (through systems thinking):** "What are the feedback loops creating tickets? How would a systems engineer see this?"

Might reveal: you're treating symptoms (tickets) instead of causes (poor onboarding, unclear docs).

**Prompt to AI:**
```
You're a [domain expert: designer/psychologist/economist/biologist].
Looking at [problem], what do you notice that I'm missing?
What would you do differently?
```

Suggested domains:
- Designer (UX, flow, emotion)
- Psychologist (behavior, incentives, fear)
- Systems thinker (feedback loops, unintended consequences)
- Economist (incentives, trade-offs, scarcity)
- Biologist (evolution, fitness, adaptation)
- Architect (structure, constraints, elegance)

---

## Lens 7: Assumptions — What am I optimizing for implicitly?

**Core idea:** Every decision has hidden optimization targets. Surface them.

**Reformulated question:**
- "What am I actually optimizing for?"
- "What trade-offs am I making implicitly?"
- "What would I have to give up to win?"
- "Is that what I actually want to optimize for?"

**When to use:**
- Clarifying goals
- Alignment checks (team, org)
- Decision-making (uncover true priorities)
- Catching misalignment

**Example:**
- **Surface:** "Should we move to a serverless architecture?"
- **Assumptions lens:** "What am I optimizing for? Cost? Speed? Flexibility? Hiring ease?"

Might reveal: you're optimizing for developer velocity, not cost. Different answer.

**Prompt to AI:**
```
What am I actually optimizing for in [decision]?
What trade-offs am I making? Is that intentional or hidden?
Help me clarify what I'm really trying to achieve.
```

---

## How to combine lenses

**Trio for clarity:**
- Lens 7 (Assumptions) — What am I really optimizing for?
- Lens 3 (Meta) — What am I not asking?
- Lens 1 (Challenge) — What's wrong with this?

**Trio for creativity:**
- Lens 5 (Inverse) — What's the opposite?
- Lens 6 (Reframe) — How would a designer see this?
- Lens 2 (Unconstrained) — What if I had unlimited resources?

**Trio for risk:**
- Lens 4 (Uncertainty) — Where am I wrong?
- Lens 5 (Inverse) — What could go catastrophically wrong?
- Lens 3 (Meta) — What risks haven't I considered?

---

## Integration with AI

**One-shot:** Run all 7 lenses at once. Get structured output. Pick what resonates.

**Iterative:** Explore one lens thoroughly, then move to the next.

**Synthesis:** After exploring 2-3 lenses, synthesize insights and suggest next steps.

**Prompt template:**
```
Run my question through these 7 lenses:
1. Challenge: What's wrong?
2. Unconstrained: What if infinite?
3. Meta: What didn't I ask?
4. Uncertainty: Where am I wrong?
5. Inverse: What's opposite?
6. Reframe: How would [expert] see it?
7. Assumptions: What am I optimizing for?

Show me the 3 most important reformulations. Help me pick which to explore next.
```
